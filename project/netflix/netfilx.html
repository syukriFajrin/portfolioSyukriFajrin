Netflix Film Data Analysis Report

Overview

This analysis explores film and TV show data available on Netflix. The objective is to understand release trends, country contributions, genre popularity, and content distribution over time.

Film Release by Year

Based on the Film Release by Year visualization:

  • Netflix content releases increased significantly after 2000.
  • Rapid growth is observed between 2015 and 2019.
  • The highest number of releases occurred around 2018–2019.
  • A slight decline appears after 2020.
  • Before 1980, very few titles were included in the dataset.

    • Monthly Release Distribution

    From the Month vs Total chart:

  • Content releases are fairly evenly distributed throughout the year.
  • July and December show slightly higher release volumes.
  • No strong seasonal dependency is observed.

    • Top 10 Countries Producing the Most Content

    This reflects Netflix’s global content diversification strategy.

    Old vs New Content Distribution

    Most content available on Netflix consists of older titles, suggesting that Netflix maintains a large content library while continuously adding new releases.

    Top 10 Most Popular Genres

  • International Movies and Drama dominate the platform.
  • Drama and Comedy remain core audience preferences.
  • International content plays a major role in Netflix’s catalog.
  • Family and romantic genres represent a smaller portion compared to drama-focused content.

    • Overall Conclusion

  • Netflix significantly expanded its content after 2015.
  • The United States remains the largest content contributor.
  • Content releases are distributed evenly throughout the year.
  • The platform contains more older titles than new releases.
  • Drama and International Movies are the dominant genres.
